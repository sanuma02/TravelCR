Corcovado National Park

Declared as a protected area since 1975 is the jewel of Costa Rica National Park system. Definitely huge with 41.800 hectares (83.600 acres) tropical rainforest, covering around 1/3 of Osa Pen√≠nsula and 5.375 hectares (10.750 acres) of marine zone.

Corcovado National Park is an undeniably beautiful place, considered to be one of the most important natural preserves in the Americas, this virgin rain forest park invites superlatives from all who visit.

There are 8 main habitats in this park, mountain forest, cloud forest, alluvial plains, swamp and yolillo (Raphia taedigera) forest, fresh water herbaceous swamp, mangrove swamp and a rocky coastline, with all this diversity make Corcovado a botanist's dream. Jaguars, anteaters, white-lipped peccaries, tapirs, inhabit the vast reaches surrounding the crocodile-filled lagoon at the park's center. The captivating visual beauty of its forest and of its coastline, with miles of uninhabited beaches and roaring cascades, draws visitors from all over the world. Also is one of the few places in the country hosting the Squirrel Monkey and the Scarlet Macaw both endangered.

With 500 species of trees, 140 species of mammals, 367 species of birds, 40 species of freshwater fishes and 117 species of amphibians recorded here, it is easy to understand why Corcovado National Park has been called "the most biologically intense place on Earth" by National Geographic. Is probably the only place in the world with more biological diversity according with the size of the park.
