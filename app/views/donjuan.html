Don Juan Eco-Farm

He is a local farmer who takes great pride in teaching visitors about tropical farming and native and non-native plants, including their medicinal, industrial and cooking uses, and health benefits. He is especially proud of his bio-digester methane gas production system that uses cow manure as a fuel source to produce methane gas for cooking! We'll tour the fields picking products, test your skills with a tortilla-making challenge and enjoy lunch prepared by local ladies, using freshly picked fruits and vegetables from the farm.

In the same property they have a “trapiche” you will see from first hand the process of sugar cane, drink it and enjoy the process sugar cane liquor: “guaro”.

A local family who are very proud of their work done in the farm: organic products. They love to teach local people about his farming and very proud about their food.

Also we can meet Chocolate and Leche, his 2 caws and his biodigestor methane gas production. He uses cow manure as a fuel source to produce the gas for his kitchen.

Part of your duties at the farm will be collecting some of the products use for your lunch, also tortillas lessons

"We started this project with a vision of creating a viable, organic, educational farm. My family and I are incredibly pleased with what we have been able to achieve up to this point. We are continually looking for ways to build the educational piece of this endeavor in the hopes of convincing others of the importance of conservation and the viability of organic practices. We hope you understand the importance of our work here.

We speak organic. Education for a Sustainable Planet!"

Sincerely: Don Juan Bautista and his family.
