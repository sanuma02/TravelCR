Irazú Volcano National Park

The park was established on July 30, 1950, with an extension of 2.309 hectares (5.100 acres).

Since 1970 national park system has the administration.

The last period of activity of this volcano was between 1963-65, huge columns of gases and ash were spewed, covering the nearby city of Cartago and central valley were San José, Heredia and Alajuela 3 of the major cities are located.

The volcano is part of the Central Volcanic Mountain Range, and is the highest active in the country, up to 3432 meters (10.900 ft).

Thanks to all the intense accumulations of volcanic material, the area is so reach, that´s why on the way to the volcano you will see farming areas growing a lot of different crops like celery, carrots, onions, garlic, beats, potatoes, this crop gives to the locals the nickname, due to the large amount of this crop they grow.

The temperature ranges between 17° C (62° F) 3°C (39°F). Rainfall average of 2.158 mts (84 inch) per year.

According to the locals the name is due to the original indigenous tribe who inhabit the area with the name of "Iztarù" means mountain of thunders and tremors.

The park has 4 craters, the main one with 1050 meters diameter and 300 meters deep and small lagoon. Diego de la Haya crater with 600 meters diameter and 100 meters deep. A big extension named Playa Hermosa was a crater long time ago, now is cover by the ash of the last explosion, the last crater is located close to the main entrance.
